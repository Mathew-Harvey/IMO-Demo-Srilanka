Sri Lanka National Technology Demonstration & Training Workshop

Strengthening biofouling management capacity in Sri Lanka — in-water inspection, biofouling risk assessment, and in-water cleaning guidance, with a live ROV inspection demonstration in the Port of Colombo.

📅 23–26 June 2026 📍 Colombo Dockyard, Colombo 🤝 IMO PCU · MTCC Africa · MEPA
Workshop Resources

Your participant toolkit

Three practical, browser-based tools used in the demonstration. No sign-in, no install — open them on your phone, tablet or laptop.

🦪

Biofouling Identification Guide

A visual field guide for spotting invasive marine species on ship hulls — Sri Lanka edition, covering species of concern such as the charru mussel and Asian green mussel.

  • Photo galleries & "how to spot it" tips
  • Sri Lanka IMS list with MEPA reporting
  • Printable A3 poster & A5 pocket booklet
Open the guide →
📊

Hull Fouling Cost Calculator

Estimate the added fuel, cost and greenhouse-gas penalty of hull fouling for a vessel — making the case for proactive biofouling management.

  • Fuel & emissions impact by fouling level
  • Based on UoM hull-scanning drag research
  • Interactive charts & scenario comparison

Demo Hull Inspection Report

The live ROV inspection report for MT Fortune at Colombo Port — executive summary, LoF ratings, niche-area findings and recommendations from the workshop demonstration.

About the Workshop

What the demonstration covers

Delivered through the IMO TEST Biofouling Project Coordination Unit (PCU), MTCC Africa (JKUAT), the Marine Environment Protection Authority (MEPA) of Sri Lanka, and international technical experts and technology providers.

🔍

In-water inspection

Methodologies and digital reporting for assessing hull and niche-area biofouling.

📋

Risk assessment

Biofouling risk assessment aligned with the IMO Biofouling Guidelines.

🧽

In-water cleaning

IWC considerations and guidelines to support informed decision-making.

🤖

Live ROV demo

Remotely Operated Vehicle inspection in a real operational setting in the Port of Colombo.

Spotted a suspect invasive species?

Keep the specimen and photos, note the vessel's recent ports, and report to the Marine Environment Protection Authority (MEPA).
